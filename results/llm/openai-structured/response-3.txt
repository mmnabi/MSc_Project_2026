To conduct a threat identification exercise using the MITRE ATT&CK Enterprise framework for NordFinans Bank AB, we will map threats across the 14 tactics in the framework. Each tactic includes relevant techniques (and their IDs) tailored to the organization’s infrastructure and business context.

---

## **1. Reconnaissance**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1595.001 - Active Scanning: Vulnerability Scanning**  
    Attackers may scan NordFinans’s web banking portal, Open Banking APIs, and external IP ranges to identify exploitable vulnerabilities, especially given the publicly accessible nature of these services.
- **T1592.002 - Gather Victim Identity Information: Employee Names**  
    Threat actors could gather employee names (e.g., from LinkedIn or other public sources) to target key personnel for phishing or social engineering.
- **T1596.001 - Search Open Technical Databases**  
    NordFinans’s use of publicly accessible APIs and Azure-hosted services increases the likelihood of attackers searching for exposed secrets, credentials, or vulnerable configurations in repositories like GitHub.

---

## **2. Resource Development**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1588.002 - Obtain Capabilities: Toolkits**  
    Threat actors may purchase or download credential-stealing malware or exploits targeting vulnerabilities in NordFinans’s public-facing systems, such as web banking or API services.
- **T1585.001 - Establish Accounts: Social Media Accounts**  
    Cybercriminals could create fake accounts on social media platforms to impersonate bank officials or departments for phishing campaigns.
- **T1587.001 - Develop Capabilities: Malware**  
    Custom malware might be developed to target the Temenos T24 system or SWIFT infrastructure.

---

## **3. Initial Access**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1190 - Exploit Public-Facing Applications**  
    Web banking, mobile app APIs, and Open Banking APIs are potential targets for exploiting vulnerabilities in public-facing systems.
- **T1566.001 - Phishing: Spearphishing Attachment**  
    Employees may be targeted with phishing emails containing malicious attachments to compromise credentials or endpoints.
- **T1133 - External Remote Services**  
    Compromising VPN access (e.g., via stolen credentials or MFA fatigue attacks) could give attackers initial access to internal systems.

---

## **4. Execution**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1059.001 - Command and Scripting Interpreter: PowerShell**  
    Threat actors could use PowerShell scripts to execute malicious payloads on Windows systems.
- **T1203 - Exploitation for Client Execution**  
    Malicious documents exploiting vulnerabilities in end-user applications (e.g., Microsoft Office) could be used to execute code on employee endpoints.

---

## **5. Persistence**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1547.001 - Boot or Logon Autostart Execution: Registry Run Keys/Startup Folder**  
    Attackers might achieve persistence on compromised endpoints by modifying registry keys.
- **T1136.001 - Create Account: Local Account**  
    New local accounts might be created on critical servers, especially in the core banking or SWIFT zones.
- **T1574.002 - Hijack Execution Flow: DLL Side-Loading**  
    Legacy Java components in the Temenos T24 system may be vulnerable to DLL side-loading to maintain persistence.

---

## **6. Privilege Escalation**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1068 - Exploitation for Privilege Escalation**  
    Exploiting vulnerabilities in older systems (e.g., legacy Java in T24) could allow attackers to escalate privileges.
- **T1078.002 - Valid Accounts: Domain Accounts**  
    Attackers with stolen credentials may escalate privileges by targeting domain admin accounts stored in CyberArk.

---

## **7. Defense Evasion**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1562.001 - Impair Defenses: Disable or Modify Tools**  
    Threat actors may attempt to disable endpoint detection and response (EDR) tools like CrowdStrike Falcon.
- **T1027 - Obfuscated Files or Information**  
    Malicious scripts or payloads may be obfuscated to evade detection by the SOC or MSSP.
- **T1070.004 - Indicator Removal on Host: File Deletion**  
    Attackers may delete logs to avoid detection, especially given the limited SOC capacity outside business hours.

---

## **8. Credential Access**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1110.003 - Brute Force: Password Spraying**  
    Attackers may attempt password spraying against VPN or Microsoft 365 accounts to bypass MFA gaps.
- **T1555.003 - Credentials from Password Stores**  
    Compromised endpoints could allow access to cached credentials in browsers or password managers.
- **T1056.001 - Input Capture: Keylogging**  
    Keylogging malware could be used to capture credentials entered into critical systems like SWIFT or T24.

---

## **9. Discovery**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1016 - System Network Configuration Discovery**  
    Attackers could map NordFinans’s segmented network zones to identify paths to the core banking or SWIFT zones.
- **T1083 - File and Directory Discovery**  
    Threat actors may search for sensitive files containing customer PII, financial data, or API secrets.
- **T1046 - Network Service Scanning**  
    Internal network scanning could identify misconfigured services or open ports for lateral movement.

---

## **10. Lateral Movement**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1021.002 - Remote Services: SMB/Windows Admin Shares**  
    Attackers could exploit Windows admin shares to move laterally between servers or endpoints.
- **T1210 - Exploitation of Remote Services**  
    Vulnerabilities in branch MPLS or IPsec tunnels could be exploited to pivot into internal systems.
- **T1570 - Lateral Tool Transfer**  
    Malicious tools or scripts may be transferred across zones for further exploitation.

---

## **11. Collection**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1530 - Data from Cloud Storage Object**  
    Sensitive customer data stored in Azure services (e.g., blobs, databases) could be targeted.
- **T1005 - Data from Local System**  
    Attackers on compromised endpoints may collect data related to customer accounts or financial transactions.
- **T1114.001 - Email Collection: Local Email**  
    Emails containing sensitive financial or regulatory information may be exfiltrated from compromised M365 accounts.

---

## **12. Command and Control**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1071.001 - Application Layer Protocol: Web Protocols**  
    NordFinans’s hybrid cloud infrastructure and reliance on HTTP/S services make it susceptible to C2 communication over web protocols.
- **T1573.001 - Encrypted Channel: Symmetric Encryption**  
    Encrypted C2 channels could bypass network monitoring tools (e.g., Sentinel or firewalls).
- **T1105 - Ingress Tool Transfer**  
    Malicious payloads or ransomware may be transferred into the environment using common protocols.

---

## **13. Exfiltration**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1041 - Exfiltration Over C2 Channel**  
    Financial or PII data may be exfiltrated through a compromised C2 channel.
- **T1567.002 - Exfiltration Over Web Service: Application Layer Protocol**  
    Customer data could be exfiltrated via Azure-hosted services or APIs.
- **T1020 - Automated Exfiltration**  
    Batch scripts may be used to exfiltrate SWIFT payment data or backups.

---

## **14. Impact**
### Relevant Techniques:
- **T1486 - Data Encrypted for Impact**  
    Ransomware could encrypt critical systems like T24 or SWIFT, impacting banking operations.
- **T1499 - Endpoint DoS**  
    Denial-of-service attacks against customer-facing platforms (web/mobile banking) could disrupt services.
- **T1565.001 - Data Manipulation: Stored Data**  
    Financial transaction data or account balances could be manipulated, leading to regulatory and operational impact.

---

This comprehensive analysis identifies threats mapped to the organization’s infrastructure and services using the MITRE ATT&CK framework, enabling NordFinans Bank AB to prioritize mitigation strategies effectively.
